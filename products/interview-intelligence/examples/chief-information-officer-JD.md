# Chief Information Officer (CIO)

**Location:** Paris, France · Hybrid (3 days onsite) · Must be France/EU work-eligible; no relocation support offered.

## About the company

We are a specialty pharmaceutical company (~2,500 employees) manufacturing and distributing specialty generics across France, Germany, and Benelux, headquartered in Paris. Our mission is expanding access to affordable specialty medicine. We operate with patient-first values, scientific rigor, regulatory integrity, and a collaborative culture.

## The role

As CIO, you will own the company's IT and digital strategy at a pivotal moment: we're building a unified data platform to support EU regulatory reporting and increasingly data-driven R&D decisions, while raising the bar on cybersecurity and compliance across a regulated, multi-country environment. You'll report to the CEO, sit on the Executive Committee, and have a dotted line to the Board's Audit & Risk Committee.

## What you'll do

- Own IT/digital strategy and roadmap for the company
- Lead cybersecurity and compliance, including GxP, GDPR, and EU MDR requirements
- Manage the IT budget (~€8M) with full P&L authority
- Oversee the Data & Analytics function
- Represent IT to the Board and Executive Committee

You'll lead a ~45-person IT organization through 5 direct reports: Head of Infrastructure, Head of Applications, Head of Cybersecurity, Head of Data & Analytics, and IT PMO Lead. Day to day, expect budget reviews, vendor management, cross-functional steering committees, incident escalations, and architecture reviews. You'll own change management, IT governance, disaster recovery/BCP, and our validated (GxP) systems — with hiring authority for your organization and vendor contract approval up to €500K.

## Your first 6–12 months

- Stand up a unified data platform for regulatory and commercial reporting
- Complete a cybersecurity maturity assessment and remediation roadmap
- Modernize the legacy ERP integration layer

## What success looks like

**12 months:** Unified data platform live for regulatory reporting; cybersecurity roadmap approved and underway.
**24 months:** Full EUDAMED compliance achieved; IT team engagement measurably improved.
**36 months:** IT recognized as a strategic enabler of new market entry, with a sustained clean audit record.

Performance is tracked through a quarterly Board IT report, audit finding trends, system uptime/incident SLAs, and an IT organization engagement survey. In the first 90 days, we expect a thorough organizational assessment and stakeholder listening tour; by 6 months, a Board-approved cybersecurity roadmap.

## Who you'll work with

You'll report to the CEO and work closely with Quality/Regulatory Affairs, Commercial, Supply Chain, and external auditors. Our IT organization is collaborative and ready to rally behind clear direction — this role is as much about aligning and energizing the team as it is about the technology roadmap.

## What we're looking for

- 10+ years of IT leadership, including 5+ years in pharma/life sciences or another GxP-regulated industry
- A track record of leading digital transformation
- Strong cybersecurity and compliance background
- Experience managing multi-country regulatory reporting systems
- Comfortable operating in ambiguity, calm under audit pressure, high EQ working with a Board

**Nice to have:** prior CIO/VP-IT experience in generics or specialty pharma, experience with EUDAMED/serialization requirements, fluent French.

## Growth & development

This role sits on the Executive Committee with a credible path to COO or continued executive-track growth, including potential expansion to a group-level CIO scope if the company grows through M&A. We offer executive coaching, direct Board exposure, and a training budget for regulatory technology.

## Benefits & how to apply

Private health insurance, meal vouchers, hybrid work, and an annual bonus. We hold a formal gender-parity target on our Executive Committee and provide unconscious-bias training company-wide.

**Process:** recruiter screen → panel interview with the CEO and CFO → case study presentation to the Executive Committee.
