# Competency Framework — Head of DevOps, Nordlys Software (fixture, fictional)

## Execution Competencies

| Competency | Why it Predicts Success | Strong Signals | Red Flags |
|---|---|---|---|
| Infrastructure Reliability Engineering | The role exists because reliability ownership is missing. | Cites incident metrics they moved; describes blameless postmortem practice; ties reliability work to business outcomes. | Talks tooling without outcomes; blames prior teams; no measurable reliability results. |
| Compliance Program Delivery | SOC 2 Type II audit lands in 5 months. | Has owned an audit end-to-end; explains control implementation pragmatically; anticipates evidence-collection burden. | Treats compliance as checkbox theater; never interacted with auditors; delegates without understanding controls. |
| CI/CD & Developer Experience | Weekly → daily deploys at stable change-failure rate is a year-one result. | Quantifies deploy-frequency improvements; frames DX as a product; balances speed with safety gates. | Advocates speed with no failure-rate guardrail; dismisses developer complaints; big-bang migration instincts. |
| Cloud Cost Management | Cloud spend currently grows faster than revenue. | Has decoupled cost from growth; names concrete levers (rightsizing, commitments, architecture); reports cost per customer. | No cost ownership experience; hand-waves "we'll optimize later"; fear of confronting spend owners. |

| Competency | Why it Predicts Success | Strong Signals | Red Flags |
|---|---|---|---|
| Team Scaling & Coaching | Team doubles from 3 to 6 across three countries. | Has hired platform engineers in a scale-up; describes ramp plans; retains people through growth. | Only inherited mature teams; vague on hiring bar; on-call burnout stories they didn't fix. |
| Written Technical Communication | Nordlys runs on RFCs before big changes. | Points to RFCs/design docs they authored; writes before arguing; concise summaries for execs. | Prefers meetings to writing; decisions live in their head; documentation as afterthought. |
| Executive Stakeholder Translation | Infra tradeoffs must land with non-technical execs. | Explains a past infra decision in business terms; comfortable defending budget; adjusts altitude to audience. | Retreats to jargon; frames execs as obstacles; cannot connect infra work to revenue or risk. |
| Hands-on Technical Depth (AWS/K8s/Terraform) | Role is ~30% hands-on; team is small. | Recent concrete contributions; reviews code/IaC credibly; knows when NOT to be hands-on. | Fully delegated for years; nostalgia without recency; or the inverse — cannot let go of the keyboard. |
