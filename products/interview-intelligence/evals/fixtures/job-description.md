# Job Description — Head of DevOps (fixture, fictional)

**Title:** Head of DevOps
**Company Name:** Nordlys Software
**Location:** Remote (EU) · quarterly on-sites in Tallinn

**Our Team and You**
Nordlys builds the logistics optimization platform behind 200+ European freight operators. Our platform team (3 engineers, growing to 6) keeps a Kubernetes-on-AWS estate running for 9 product squads. You will own that team's mission: make shipping software at Nordlys fast, safe, and boringly reliable. You'll work daily with the CTO and the Heads of Backend, Frontend, and Data, and your decisions will shape how 60+ engineers work every day.

**The Scope of the Role and Why It's Open**
Infrastructure ownership today is split between two overloaded senior engineers; a recent 14-hour partial outage showed the cost of that gap. You will take ownership of platform reliability, developer experience, infrastructure cost, and our SOC 2 Type II journey (audit in ~5 months), while growing the team from 3 to 6.

**30 / 60 / 90-day success plan (ATR)**
- **30 days — Action:** own incident process and on-call. **Tasks:** audit current AWS/Terraform estate, meet all squad leads, ship one visible DX improvement. **Results:** incident runbook v1 adopted; on-call rotation restructured.
- **60 days — Action:** own the SOC 2 workstream. **Tasks:** gap analysis with auditor, control implementation plan, 2 platform hires in pipeline. **Results:** audit-readiness plan signed off by CTO.
- **90 days — Action:** own the infra roadmap. **Tasks:** deploy-pipeline overhaul proposal (weekly → daily deploys), cost-observability baseline. **Results:** daily deploys piloted in 2 squads at stable change-failure rate; cloud cost per customer trending down.

**What We've Achieved and What We'll Do With You**
Series B, 60% YoY growth, 140 people, engineering culture built on written RFCs and low-ego directness. With you, we scale from 4 to 9 squads without scaling chaos.

**A Little Bit About You**
You've run a platform team of 3+ through scale-up growth and can point to measurable outcomes: deploy frequency you raised, an audit you passed, an on-call you made humane. You're hands-on about 30% of the time in AWS, Kubernetes, and Terraform, and you write decisions down before you argue them. You explain infrastructure tradeoffs to non-technical executives without drama, and you treat reliability as a product with users, not a cost center.
